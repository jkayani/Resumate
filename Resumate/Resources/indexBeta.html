Hi, welcome to Resumate: Automate Your Resume! This is the all-in-one tool to help you build your resume, apply for jobs, and nail your interview. It's even split into 3 sections: Resume, Apply, and Learn. To switch between these sections, click on the tabs on the top. Above this help box, you'll see 4 buttons:
  • Options, which will open an options pane
  • Demo, which will create a demo resume
  • Reset, which will clear out the resume
  • Help, which will bring up this dialog
  • About, which will give you more information about our team

For more information on a particular section, hover over the input box and a tooltip will appear. Use these tooltips to guide you as you build your resume. In the options pane, you have 4 sections:
  • Sorting, which allows you to sort parts of your resume based on the duration of each entry
  • Fonts, which allow you to modify the font used in the resume
  • Presets,which allow you to choose a preset designed for a particular career field to customize your resume
  • Export, which gives you options for exporting your resume

Once you have filled in your information, click the big "Add to Resume" button at the bottom of the screen!

In the Apply tab, type keywords related to your desired career in the search field and choose whether you want to search for public jobs, private jobs, or both. Once you click search, it will come up with a list of available jobs, where you can get information at a glance, and even apply using the "Learn More" link.

In the Learn tab, you'll get tons of useful information on how to master your interview. It's broken up into three sections, so you'll know what to do before, during, and after your interview.

Quick Tips to Ace Your Interview


Part 1: Before the interview

  • Put the date and time in your phone, on your wall calendar, wherever you will remember!
  • Make sure you have the professional clothes and they are clean and wrinkle free.
  • Do lots of research and memorize their job listing so you know exactly what the are looking for
  • Leave early so you arrive on time!

Part 2: During the Interview

  • Start off the interview with a firm handshake
  • Stay calm - they want to hire you just as much as you want to be hired!
  • Speak confidently and keep your responses succinct

Common interview questions and answers:


Q: Tell me about your experience at [job on resume]
A: Make sure your answer mentions experiences you know will be useful at this new job. Also, don't badmouth you're previous employer!


Q: What is your biggest professional accomplishment so far?
A: Pick the accomplish that seems most professional from the outside, and use a statistic if you can (25% improvement, etc.) Again, make sure it's relevant to this new job!


Q: How would your coworkers describe you?
A: With this question, you want to show that you work well with others. Focus on group projects you made bi contributions to and worked as a team on.


Q: What is your greatest weakness?
A: The classic interview question! You want to answer this wuestion srategicly. Name a weakness that can also be a strength, like being a perfectionist, or being frustrated when when someone doesn't get their work done on time.

Q: Why are ou the best person for this job? A: You did your research, right? for this question you want to think back to the job posting and mentions some of the keywords they're looking for in a candidate.


Part 3: After the Interview


The most important thing after you finish the interview is to send a thank-you or follow-up letter. Most candidates don't send one, so sending one can go a long way! You send send a letter even if you know you are getting the job or ou are sure someone else will get it. Keep your thank-you letter short and make sure to thank your interviewer for their time. Show that you are really excited for this particular position, even if it is just one of msny jobs you are applying for.
